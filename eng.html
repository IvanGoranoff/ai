What is AI-Artificial Intelligence?

  • History

    Artificial intelligence is increasingly playing an important role in our lives, with the latest trend being AI processors in our smartphones. This technology began to evolve in the 1950s with the Dartmouth Summer Research Project of Dartmouth College in the United States.

     

    He became popular with the work of Alan Turing, to whom the famous Turing test belongs. But AI has come under the spotlight on the world stage with the advent of IBM's Deep Blue chess supercomputer, which was the first machine to beat world chess champion Gary Kasparov in a match in 1996.

       

    Artificial intelligence algorithms have been used in data centers and big computers for many years, but are only now available in consumer electronics.

  • Definition of artificial intelligence

    The definition of artificial intelligence characterizes it as a branch of computer science that deals with the automation of intelligent behavior. The difficulty comes from the fact that it is very difficult to accurately determine intelligence, and therefore artificial intelligence cannot be well defined either.

    Generally, the term is used to describe systems designed to use machines to emulate and simulate human intelligence and behavior. This can be achieved through simple algorithms and predefined models, but it can also be much more complicated.

  • Different types of AI

    Symbolic AI systems work with abstract symbols that are used to represent knowledge. The information is processed from above by working with human-readable characters, abstract links and logical conclusions.               

                   

                   Neural Artificial Intelligence became popular in computer science in the late 1980s. Here, knowledge is not represented by symbols, but rather by artificial neurons and their connections - something like a reconstructed brain. The knowledge gathered is divided into small pieces - neurons - and then connected and built into groups. Unlike symbolic AI, the neural system must be trained and stimulated so that the neural networks gain experience and grow, and thus accumulate more knowledge.

  • Methods and tools

    There are various tools and methods for applying artificial intelligence in real-world scenarios, some of which can be used in parallel. The basis of all this is machine learning, which is defined as a system that builds knowledge from experience. Machine learning uses symbolic and neural AI.               

                  

    There are three learning processes for learning neural networks: learning under control, unsupervised, and validation training, providing many different ways to regulate how the desired result is delivered.

  • Uses

                Artificial intelligence is already used in many areas, but by no means all of them are visible at first glance. Artificial Intelligence mechanisms are great for detecting, identifying and classifying objects and faces on photos and videos. Chatbots are increasingly used in customer service. These assistants recognize keywords that the customer can say and respond to at their set value.               

                  

                  Search algorithms like Google are naturally top secret. But the way in which the search results are calculated, measured and output is largely determined by the mechanisms that work with machine learning.               

                  

    Word and its grammar and spell checker has been a classic symbolic AI application that has been used for a long time. Language is defined as a complex network of rules and instructions that analyze blocks of text in a sentence and, in certain circumstances, can identify and correct errors.

  • AI in medicine

                  Artificial intelligence in medicine and healthcare is based on the use of automatic and self-learning processes in the diagnosis and treatment of patients in need of medical attention.

    Artificial intelligence in medicine and healthcare has been a particularly hot topic in recent years. The argument in support of artificial intelligence is that it will automate much of these areas. Although there are some fears that this will lead to the loss of human regard in such an important profession, in fact automation, proponents of the concept say, will hasten the completion of tasks, which will allow specialists to spend more time with their patients. According to studies from several years ago, physicians spend far more time entering data and bureaucracy than actually engaging with people - the latter equates to about 27% of the workday.               

  • AI in industrial production

                 In order to remain competitive in today's global market, manufacturers face the challenge of optimizing all possible aspects of their business - from the supply chain, through resource and procurement management, to product design, logistics and services. This need, along with the coming fourth industrial revolution in manufacturing systems, necessitates a total rethinking of business goals and the means to achieve them. The help of industrial enterprises is the big data constantly generated from connected factory equipment, as well as revolutionary technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning, which help to extract value from these information arrays and gradually become invariable. part of industrial automation production platforms.

    Among the benefits of this new technological line are the possibilities for predictive maintenance, higher production efficiency, advanced quality control, optimized in character and duration of design and development process, reduction of losses of materials and resources, etc.

  • The future of AI

    AI is becoming ubiquitous and is now more than just a modern word. Many businesses have focused in this direction and are trying to create innovation. "Ten years ago, no one could afford artificial intelligence," says Rajgopal, pointing to the prohibitively high price. With the advent of commercial AI software, this technology is already emerging from the realm of luxury.

    "The exponential technology curve will continue over the next five years. You will have insanely powerful virtual assistants, automated cars, genetically targeted drugs, and the like. In 10 years, we will be like cute pets for our AI masters, "says Kapps.

AI in photography

AI smartphone cameras - a revolution in photography


Today, 85% of the world's photos are taken with smartphones (according to Infotrends statistics). Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be trained to become a better photographer than most people could ever be. Also, what if we could learn AI in all types of photography? This is great potential for AI photography: to gather photographic knowledge from the past 100 years and apply it to user photos, automatically or on demand. AI can help everyone take and share amazing photos at any time.


AI cameras are powered by sophisticated artificial intelligence software to gather visual information about what the person behind the camera wants to capture. Once that happens, AI can optimize the camera settings in an optimal way based on the built-in knowledge and skills. The AI camera does the job that an experienced photographer would do manually.

Google Lens

Google Lens

            

           Google Lens is a tool that allows us to scan various objects with the camera and receive a variety of information about them. For example, we can find out what kind of a plant is or learn more about architectural sites and more. The new feature uses machine learning algorithms to recognize and understand what it sees in front of itself and provide up-to-date information about the objects themselves. If you want to check if Google Lens is available to you, you need to go to Google Photos and click on a photo. The middle of the image should have a new Google Lens icon from which you can find out more about the subject you were shooting.
